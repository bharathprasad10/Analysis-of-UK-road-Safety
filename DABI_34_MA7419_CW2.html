1. Analysis based on Age bands and casualty class (2016 - 2020)

  • Three casualty classes - Driver/rider, Passenger, Pedestrian
  • Number of casualties highest in age band 26-35, followed by 36-45
  • Most casualties above 18 years of age are drivers/riders
  • Driving test should be intensified for the age-groups with highest casualties
  • Usage of public transport must be encourage and rules governing private ownership of vehicles must be stringent

3. Number of accidents every hour per week day (2016-2020)

  • Casualties peak during start and end of working hours, i.e., 08:00 and from 15:00 - 18:00
  • Minimum during early mornings and nights
  • Road users should be alerted, stricter Road Traffic Police patrolling during busy hours

4. Road type and speed limit

4. Road type and speed limit

  • maximum accidents with casualties occur in single carriageways with speed limit 30mph
  • Mostly because of lack of lane dividers and/or insufficient light
  • second maximum accident count in dual carriageways, speed limit 70 mph
  • Solutions: Prompt accident response, improved visibility of signs and reflectors

5. Accident factors
